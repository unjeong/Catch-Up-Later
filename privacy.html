Privacy Policy for Catch Up Later

Last Updated: December 28, 2025

Overview

Catch Up Later is a Chrome extension designed to help users efficiently monitor updates from Gmail, YouTube, Google Drive, RSS feeds, and websites. We value your privacy and are committed to protecting your sensitive data through strict security protocols and a "Client-Side Only" architecture.

Google API Disclosure (Limited Use)

Catch Up Later's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Specifically, data accessed via Google APIs is used only to provide or improve user-facing features and is not used for advertising, serving ads, or any other purposes not explicitly authorized by the user.

Information We Access

1. Google User Data (Sensitive Scopes)

We only access data after you provide explicit consent via the Google OAuth 2.0 screen:

  • Gmail (https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.modify & .readonly): We access email metadata (sender, subject, snippets) to show unread counts. We do NOT store your email content on any server.
  • YouTube (https://www.googleapis.com/auth/youtube.readonly): We access your subscription list to notify you of new video uploads.
  • Google Drive (https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.metadata.readonly): We access metadata of shared files to help you track recent updates.

2. Local Data

  • URLs of monitored websites and RSS feeds.
  • Extension configuration and notification preferences.

Data Security & Protection Mechanisms

To address the protection of sensitive data, Catch Up Later implements the following mechanisms:

  • Encryption in Transit: All communications between the extension and Google APIs are conducted exclusively over industry-standard HTTPS (TLS 1.2/1.3), ensuring that data is encrypted while moving between your device and Google's servers.
  • No External Server Storage: We do NOT maintain any external databases or servers. All sensitive authentication tokens and fetched metadata are stored locally in your browser's secure sandbox using the chrome.storage.local API.
  • Sandboxed Environment: Chrome extensions run in an isolated environment, preventing other websites or applications from accessing the sensitive data managed by Catch Up Later.
  • Human Access Restriction: Since no data is transmitted to us, no human at Catch Up Later can view, access, or process your personal Google information.

How We Use Your Information

Data usage is strictly limited to the following functional purposes:

  • Displaying a unified dashboard of your unread updates.
  • Generating local browser notifications for new content.
  • Providing a one-click "Mark as Read" feature for Gmail.

Data Sharing

We do NOT share, sell, or rent your personal data to any third parties. Data is never used for marketing, advertising, or creditworthiness assessments. Your information only moves between your browser and the official Google API endpoints.

Data Retention & Deletion

  • Temporary Local Storage: Fetched metadata is kept temporarily in local storage to provide the service.
  • User-Initiated Deletion: You can wipe all stored data by signing out within the extension or removing a monitored source.
  • Full Removal: Uninstalling the Catch Up Later extension from Chrome immediately and permanently deletes all locally stored configuration and cached data.

Your Rights

You maintain full control over your data. You may revoke access at any time through your Google Security Settings.
